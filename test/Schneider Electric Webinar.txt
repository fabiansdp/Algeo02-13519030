How to Prepare for an Interview
1. Perform research on the company and role
	- Understand product or services
	- Understand job description
	- Fit within company culture

2. Prepare answers
	- Please tell me about yourself
	- Why do you want to work here
	- What interests you about this role?
	- Strength weakness

3. Practice
	- Confident, strong, speaking voice and friendly
	- Ask questions
	- Conduct mock interview

4. Prepare your travel arrangements
	- Leave early
	- Save interview information
	- Search location in advance

5. Follow up after the interview
	- First paragraph, mention specific job and thank interviewer
	- Second, note a conversation point that seemed especially important

Star Technique
A. Situation
Sample Response : Advertising revenue was falling off for my college newspaper, The Review, and large numbers of long-term advertisers were not renewing contracts. 

B. Task
Sample Response : My goal was to generate new ideas, materials and incentives that would result in at least a 15% increase in advertisers from the year before.

C. Action
Sample Response: I designed a new promotional packet to go with the rate sheet and compared the benefits of The Review circulation with other ad media in the area. I also set up a special training session for the account executives with a School of Business Administration professor who discussed competitive selling strategies.

D. Result
Sample Response: We signed contract with 15 former advertisers for daily ads and five for special supplements. We increased our new advertisers by 20 percent over the same period last year.
